## Pricing Strategies

### 1. Value Leader Strategy

**Goal:** Lowest prices in market
- Attract price-sensitive customers
- High volume, lower margins
- Compete on price

**Who uses:** High-volume dispensaries, discount brands

**Example:**
```
Market average: $40
Your price: $35 (-12.5%)
Position: Value leader

Pros:
- Attracts budget shoppers
- High sales volume
- Takes market share

Cons:
- Lower profit margins
- Race to bottom
- Harder to raise prices later
```

### 2. Premium Strategy

**Goal:** Higher prices, premium positioning
- Attract quality-conscious customers
- Lower volume, higher margins
- Compete on quality/experience

**Who uses:** Boutique brands, craft cultivators

**Example:**
```
Market average: $40
Your price: $48 (+20%)
Position: Premium

Pros:
- Higher profit per sale
- Premium brand perception
- Better customer lifetime value

Cons:
- Lower sales volume
- Need to justify premium (quality, service)
- Smaller addressable market
```

### 3. Match-the-Market Strategy

**Goal:** Price competitively, compete on other factors
- Match average market price
- Compete on quality, service, convenience
- Balanced approach

**Who uses:** Most brands

**Example:**
```
Market average: $40
Your price: $40 (0%)
Position: Competitive

Pros:
- Price not a barrier
- Focus on differentiation
- Flexibility to adjust

Cons:
- No price advantage
- Must excel elsewhere
```

### 4. Dynamic Pricing Strategy

**Goal:** Adjust prices based on demand, competition, inventory
- Maximize revenue per product
- Optimize for market conditions
- Automated price adjustments

**Who uses:** Data-driven brands (with Ledger agent)

**Example:**
```
Product: Blue Dream 3.5g
Base price: $40

Dynamic adjustments:
- High demand, low inventory: $42 (+5%)
- Low demand, high inventory: $38 (-5%)
- Competitor dropped price: $38 (match)
- Weekend peak demand: $42 (+5%)
- Monday slow day: $38 (-5%)

Result: 12% higher revenue vs. static pricing
```

## Best Practices

<Callout type="tip">
**Don't race to bottom:** Being cheapest isn't always best. Focus on value, not just price.

**Monitor regularly:** Check competitive pricing weekly. Markets shift quickly.

**Test price changes:** A/B test price increases. Track impact on volume and profit.

**Communicate value:** If charging premium, explain why (quality, service, experience).

**Use dynamic pricing:** Automate price adjustments with Ledger for maximum profit.
</Callout>
