## Types of QR Codes

### 1. Full Menu QR Code

**Links to complete product catalog**

**Use cases:**
- Table tents in waiting area
- Window displays
- Printed flyers
- Business cards

**Example:**
```
[QR Code]

Scan to view our full menu!
Order online for pickup or delivery

yourbrand.markitbot.com/menu
```

### 2. Product-Specific QR Codes

**Links to individual product page**

**Use cases:**
- Product jar labels
- Shelf tags
- Display cases
- Product samplesExample:**
```
[QR Code]

Blue Dream 3.5g
Scan for details, reviews & to order

yourbrand.markitbot.com/product/blue-dream
```

### 3. Promotion QR Codes

**Links to specific sale or offer**

**Use cases:**
- Sale flyers
- Email campaigns
- Social media posts
- In-store signage

**Example:**
```

## Dynamic vs. Static QR Codes

### Static QR Codes

**URL hardcoded in QR:**
- Cannot change destination after printing
- Works forever (no expiration)
- No tracking
- Smaller file size

**Best for:**
- Permanent URLs (brand website)
- Long-term use
- No analytics needed

### Dynamic QR Codes (Markitbot default)

**URL redirects through Markitbot:**
- Can change destination URL after printing
- Tracking and analytics
- Can expire (set expiration date)
- Larger file size (slightly)

**Best for:**
- Promotions (might change offer)
- Products (page URL might change)
- Events (link becomes inactive after)
- Anything you want to track

**Example:**
```

## QR Code Security

**Best practices:**
- Use HTTPS URLs only (secure)
- Don't link to suspicious sites
- Test destination before printing
- Monitor for unusual scan patterns (bot attacks)
