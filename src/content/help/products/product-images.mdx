## Types of Product Photos

### 1. Hero Shot (Primary)

**Main product image**
- Clean, well-lit
- White or neutral background
- Product centered, fills frame
- Sharp focus, high resolution

**Use:** Main product page image, catalog listings

### 2. Detail Shots

**Close-ups showing:**
- Flower trichomes (macro photography)
- Product texture
- Packaging details
- Lab results label

**Use:** Product page gallery (2-4 additional images)

### 3. Lifestyle/Context Shots

**Product in use or setting:**
- Flower next to grinder
- Vape with battery
- Edibles on plate
- Product in hand (scale reference)

**Use:** Marketing materials, social media

### 4. Packaging Shots

**Sealed product packaging:**
- Compliant child-resistant packaging
- Brand label visible
- Unopened/sealed

**Use:** E-commerce (what customer receives)

### Camera Settings (DSLR)

**Recommended:**
- Mode: Aperture Priority (Av) or Manual (M)
- Aperture: f/8-f/11 (sharp focus, full product in focus)

- White Balance: 5500K or Auto

### Smartphone Settings

**Tips:**
- Use native camera app (better than third-party)
- Tap to focus on product
- Lock exposure (AE/AF Lock)
- Use grid (rule of thirds)
- Shoot in well-lit area
- Clean lens before shooting

## Shooting Process

### Step 1: Prepare Product

**Flower:**
- Select photogenic bud (symmetrical, frosty)
- Remove stems, shake off loose material
- Use tweezers to position perfectly

**Packaged products:**
- Clean packaging (no fingerprints, dust)
- Label facing camera
- Remove price stickers

### Step 2: Compose Shot

**Rule of thirds:**
- Place product at intersection points
- Leave breathing room
- Don't crop tootight

**Angles:**
- **Straight-on** (hero shot)
- **45-degree** (shows depth)
- **Top-down** (flat lays)
- **Close-up** (details)

### Step 3: Capture Multiple Shots

**Take 5-10 photos per product:**
- Different angles
- Various distances
- Adjust lighting between shots
- Select best later

## Editing Product Photos

### Basic Edits (Required)

**Using Lightroom or photo editor:**

1. **Crop & straighten**
   - 1:1 square ratio (product pages)
   - Product centered
   - Remove distractions

2. **Adjust exposure**
   - Increase if too dark
   - Decrease if too bright
   - Target: white background = pure white

3. **White balance**
   - Correct color cast
   - Whites should be neutral (not blue/yellow)

4. **Sharpen**
   - Add clarity (details pop)
   - Don't over-sharpen (looks fake)

5. **Clean up**
   - Remove dust specs
   - Clone out imperfections
   - Healing brush tool

### Advanced Edits (Optional)

**For professional results:**

1. **Background removal**
   - Remove.bg (free online tool)
   - Photoshop (pen tool, layer mask)
   - Pure white or transparent background

2. **Color correction**
   - Vibrance +10-20
   - Saturation +5-10
   - Avoid over-saturated (looks fake)

3. **Shadows & highlights**
   - Lift shadows (show detail)
   - Recover highlights (prevent blowout)

4. **Spot corrections**
   - Remove distracting elements
   - Clean up messy backgrounds

## Cannabis Photography Challenges

### Challenge 1: Trichomes

**Problem:** Hard to capture frost/crystals

**Solution:**
- Macro lens or macro mode
- Side lighting (shows texture)
- Black background (contrast)
- Focus stacking (multiple shots, merge)

### Challenge 2: Color Accuracy

**Problem:** Purple strains look blue, greens look off

**Solution:**
- Shoot RAW format (more color data)
- Custom white balance (gray card)
- Color checker (X-Rite ColorChecker)
- Manual color correction in post

### Challenge 3: Consistency

**Problem:** Photos look different across products

**Solution:**
- Same lighting setup for all products
- Same background
- Same camera settings
- Batch edit (apply edits to all)

## Sourcing Product Images

### Option 1: Shoot Yourself

**Pros:**
- Full control
- Unique to your brand
- No licensing issues

**Cons:**
- Time-consuming
- Requires equipment/skills
- Learning curve

### Option 2: Brand-Provided

**Request from suppliers:**
- Many brands provide product photos
- Usually high-quality
- Free to use (with permission)

**Ask for:**
- Hero shots
- Detail shots
- Transparent background versions

### Option 3: Stock Photography

**Cannabis stock photo sites:**
- Shutterstock (cannabis section)
- Adobe Stock
- iStock
- Unsplash (some free cannabis images)

**Caution:**
- Generic, not your actual product
- Other brands may use same photo
- Licensing costs

### Option 4: Hire Professional

**Cannabis product photographer:**
- $500-2,000 per day shoot
- 50-100 products per session
- Edited, ready-to-use

**Find photographers:**
- Local cannabis photographers (Google)
- Instagram (#cannabisphotography)
- Upwork/Fiverr (freelance)

3. **Clean lens** (use microfiber cloth)
4. **Use grid** (Settings ‚Üí Camera ‚Üí Grid)
5. **HDR mode** (better dynamic range)

**Recommended apps:**
